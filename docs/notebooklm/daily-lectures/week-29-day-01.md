## 2. Mini lecture
The week focus is Critical infrastructure, governance, risk, and assessment mindset, and today's objective is kick off the week's topic and build the right vocabulary. The main terms in scope are Critical Infrastructure and Business Impact Analysis. The focus is on what each one does, how it operates, and how it affects the rest of the system.

Critical Infrastructure is systems essential to societal and economic stability. It includes essential services whose disruption would significantly affect safety, economy, health, or national security. Mechanically, it depends on interlocking services, suppliers, and control systems, so failure in one area can cascade into others. Business Impact Analysis is process for identifying operational effects of disruption over time. It estimates process disruption effects, recovery priorities, and tolerable downtime for critical business functions. Mechanically, it maps business processes to dependencies, outage tolerances, and recovery needs so disruption consequences can be measured. Together, these terms describe where work happens, how state changes, and what the visible outcome looks like when the system is operating normally.
