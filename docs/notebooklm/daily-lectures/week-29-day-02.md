# Governance and Compliance in context

## 2. Mini lecture
The week focus is Critical infrastructure, governance, risk, and assessment mindset, and today's objective is deepen concepts and connect them to the bigger system. The main terms in scope are Governance and Compliance. The focus is on what each one does, how it operates, and how it affects the rest of the system.

Governance is decision framework defining accountability, policy, and oversight. It assigns decision rights, policy direction, and oversight mechanisms for managing cybersecurity activities. Mechanically, it assigns decision rights, accountability, and policy direction so security work aligns with organizational objectives. Compliance is conformance to laws, regulations, standards, or internal policies. It measures implemented practices against external or internal requirements and documents conformity or deficiency. Mechanically, it compares actual practices and evidence against a required standard, then records where controls meet or miss expectations. Together, these terms describe where work happens, how state changes, and what the visible outcome looks like when the system is operating normally.

Translate this into governance language: assets, dependencies, controls, risk owners, and oversight. The goal is to connect the concept to actual behavior, output, or system state.
