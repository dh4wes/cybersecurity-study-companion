Mobile Device means portable endpoint such as a phone, tablet, or lightweight computer with integrated radios and battery power. Its practical job is that it delivers computing, connectivity, and local apps in a form factor built for movement and short sessions. Mechanically, it combines processor, storage, battery, sensors, and wireless interfaces so one compact device can manage apps and data on the move. Laptop means portable computer with an integrated display, keyboard, battery, and mobile-oriented internal components. Its practical job is that it provides a full personal computing platform that can run unplugged and travel easily. Mechanically, it combines processor, memory, storage, battery, display, and wireless hardware in one chassis so the system operates as one endpoint. Docking Station means accessory that expands a portable computer with extra ports, displays, power, or peripherals through one connection. Its practical job is that it lets a mobile system switch quickly between portable and desk-based work setups. Mechanically, it carries power, video, USB, and network traffic through one dock link so attached peripherals reconnect as a group. Notice the pattern: the exam target here is not memorizing isolated labels, but recognizing which part of the system is responsible for speed, persistence, coordination, communication, or control.
