LAN means local area network that connects devices across a limited physical space such as a room, office, or campus. Its practical job is that it provides high-speed local communication among nearby systems and shared resources. Mechanically, it uses local switching and addressing so devices exchange frames directly across a contained network segment. WAN means wide area network that connects distant sites across provider infrastructure or large geographic areas. Its practical job is that it carries traffic between remote networks that cannot be joined by one local switching domain. Mechanically, it uses routed links across carrier or internet paths so separate local networks can exchange traffic over distance. Notice the pattern: the exam target here is not memorizing isolated labels, but recognizing which part of the system is responsible for speed, persistence, coordination, communication, or control.
