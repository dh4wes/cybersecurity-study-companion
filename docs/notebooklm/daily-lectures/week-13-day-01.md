CIA Triad means security model built around confidentiality, integrity, and availability. Its practical job is that it provides a basic framework for judging what a control is protecting or what a failure affected. Mechanically, it evaluates whether data stays secret, unaltered, and reachable, then uses those goals to choose and review controls. Risk means possibility that a threat will exploit a weakness and cause harm to an asset or process. Its practical job is that it helps teams prioritize which security problems deserve treatment first. Mechanically, it combines the chance of a harmful event with the likely impact so work can be ranked and resourced. Notice the pattern: the exam target here is not memorizing isolated labels, but recognizing which part of the system is responsible for speed, persistence, coordination, communication, or control. When two concepts seem similar, ask three questions: where does the data live, who acts on it, and what changes if this part fails. That framing turns vocabulary into diagnosis.

## 4. Key distinctions and common confusions
- CIA Triad vs Risk — CIA Triad: Security model built around confidentiality, integrity, and availability. Risk: Possibility that a threat will exploit a weakness and cause harm to an asset or process.
- Risk vs Vulnerability — Risk: Possibility that a threat will exploit a weakness and cause harm to an asset or process. Vulnerability: Weakness in design, implementation, configuration, or process that can be exploited.
- Purpose vs mechanism — be able to separate what CIA Triad is for from how it actually performs that job.
- Recognition vs diagnosis — knowing the term name is not enough; know what evidence would make you pick it in a scenario.
