CIA Triad means security model built around confidentiality, integrity, and availability. Its practical job is that it provides a basic framework for judging what a control is protecting or what a failure affected. Mechanically, it evaluates whether data stays secret, unaltered, and reachable, then uses those goals to choose and review controls. Risk means possibility that a threat will exploit a weakness and cause harm to an asset or process. Its practical job is that it helps teams prioritize which security problems deserve treatment first. Mechanically, it combines the chance of a harmful event with the likely impact so work can be ranked and resourced. Notice the pattern: the exam target here is not memorizing isolated labels, but recognizing which part of the system is responsible for speed, persistence, coordination, communication, or control.
