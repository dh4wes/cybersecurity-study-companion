## 4. Key distinctions and common confusions
- Risk Register vs Risk Assessment — Risk Register: Documented inventory of identified risks, ratings, owners, and treatments. Risk Assessment: Structured evaluation of risk likelihood, impact, and treatment options.
- Likelihood vs Impact — Likelihood: Estimated probability that a risk event will occur. Impact: Estimated consequence if a risk event occurs.
- Impact vs Likelihood — Impact: Estimated consequence if a risk event occurs. Likelihood: Estimated probability that a risk event will occur.
- Risk Treatment vs Mitigation — Risk Treatment: Chosen approach to address risk: mitigate, transfer, avoid, or accept. Mitigation: Action that lowers likelihood or impact of a risk.
- Qualitative Risk Analysis vs Quantitative Risk Analysis — Qualitative Risk Analysis: Risk assessment using categorical scales like low/medium/high. Quantitative Risk Analysis: Risk assessment using numerical estimates such as expected loss values.
