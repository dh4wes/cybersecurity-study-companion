Change Management means controlled process for proposing, reviewing, approving, implementing, and validating a change. Its practical job is that it reduces unintended outages and security regressions when systems or settings are modified. Mechanically, it records the request, evaluates risk and approval, applies the change in a planned window, and verifies the result afterward. Troubleshooting Methodology means structured step-by-step process for diagnosing and resolving technical issues. Its practical job is that it follows defined stages to identify symptoms, test causes, implement fixes, and verify normal operation. Mechanically, it defines a sequence of observe, hypothesize, test, and verify so problems are isolated without random changes.
