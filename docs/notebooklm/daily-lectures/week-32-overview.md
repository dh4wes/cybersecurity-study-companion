## Why this week matters
Week 32 sits in the Buffer / Final Review phase and focuses on catch-up, comprehensive review, and study-system reset. The deliverable is completion checklist + retrospective, which forces the learner to turn study into something explainable and reviewable. The checkpoint is everything is either complete or deliberately parked with a next step, so the week is successful only if the learner can speak clearly about system behavior rather than repeat isolated definitions. This week is synthesis-oriented rather than objective-heavy. It consolidates prior material, strengthens recall, and prepares the learner to explain earlier concepts more clearly. Across the week, the pattern moves from vocabulary and recognition into mechanism, then into examples, practice, and synthesis. That progression matters because certification-style questions usually reward the learner who can trace cause and effect, contrast similar concepts, and choose the next best action from context.

## Exam relevance
Catch-up, comprehensive review, and study-system reset helps with questions that ask the learner to identify the correct component, protocol, workflow step, or control, explain likely causes from a short scenario, and distinguish similar concepts under time pressure.

## Do not overclaim
This week is intentionally consolidation-heavy. It strengthens recall and articulation but should not be treated as a dense block of new objective coverage.
