## 2. Mini lecture
The week focus is Google Course 1: Foundations of Cybersecurity, and today's objective is deepen concepts and connect them to the bigger system. The main terms in scope are Threat and Vulnerability. The focus is on what each one does, how it operates, and how it affects the rest of the system.

Threat is potential source of harm that could exploit a weakness or disrupt normal operations. It explains who or what might cause damage, theft, misuse, or outage. Mechanically, it becomes actionable when a capable actor or condition meets an exposed weakness and a path to the target. Vulnerability is weakness in design, implementation, configuration, or process that can be exploited. It creates an opportunity for a threat to bypass protection or cause harm. Mechanically, it appears when a flaw or misconfiguration leaves a reachable gap that an attacker or failure condition can abuse. Together, these terms describe where work happens, how state changes, and what the visible outcome looks like when the system is operating normally.
